BTS

ABOUT

BTS, also known as the Bangtan Boys, is a South Korean boy band formed in 2010. The band consists of Jin, Suga, J-Hope, RM, Jimin, V, and Jungkook, who co-write or co-produce much of their material. BTS debuted on June 13, 2013, with their single album 2 Cool 4 Skool. They have since released six studio albums, three compilation albums, three extended plays, and numerous singles. BTS has won numerous awards and recognition for their music, including the Billboard Music Award for Top Social Artist for three consecutive years. The group is known for their elaborate and intricate dance choreography, which is often inspired by contemporary dance and martial arts. BTS is also active in philanthropy and has worked with the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) on their "Love Myself" campaign to end violence against children and young people.

The group's name, "BTS", stands for "Bangtan Sonyeondan" which translates to "Bulletproof Boy Scouts" in English. The members of BTS all have different areas of expertise, ranging from rapping and singing to producing and composing music. RM, the leader of BTS, is a polyglot and can speak several languages, including Korean, English, and Japanese. J-Hope is a trained dancer and was a member of a street dance team before joining BTS. V is a talented actor and has appeared in several television dramas. Jungkook is the youngest member of BTS and is known for his powerful vocals. BTS has a large and dedicated fan base known as ARMY. BTS is one of the most popular K-pop groups in the world and has broken numerous records. BTS is a global phenomenon and has inspired fans all over the world. They are a positive force in the music industry and are using their platform to make a difference.
